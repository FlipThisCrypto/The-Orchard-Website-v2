# Positioning & Category (2036)

> Canonical product positioning for The Orchard v2. Integrated by the Lead from ChatGPT's
> deliverable **ORCH-010** (`council/chatgpt/submissions/ORCH-010-v1.md`).
> **Status: PROPOSED — pending Richard's ratification.** Date: 2026-06-17.

## Recommended positioning (2036)

> **By 2036, The Orchard is the community-owned environmental data infrastructure where people
> plant privacy-preserving Trees, verify real-world conditions through open hardware and
> Chia-backed proofs, and turn local sensing into a shared data network rewarded by $JUICE.**

Why it's strongest: it anchors on what's durable — real hardware, verifiable data,
privacy-preserving location, open-source participation, and incentives that *support* the network
rather than define it. It works for crypto-native adopters (names Trees, Chia-backed proofs,
$JUICE) **and** for later institutional audiences (the center of gravity is trusted environmental
data infrastructure, not token upside). It stays honest at ~3 Trees today while pointing at the
100,000-Tree design horizon.

## Category to own

**Community-owned environmental data infrastructure.**

Broad enough for a decade, specific enough to avoid generic DePIN language. "Community-owned"
separates us from centralized weather/sensor/data platforms; "environmental data" stays legible to
universities, cities, researchers, farmers, and insurers; "infrastructure" signals that the durable
network is the product. **Trade-off:** less instantly crypto-native than "environmental DePIN" —
which remains a useful descriptor for early adopters and ecosystem context. The category we *own*
should be one a city, school, researcher, or grower can understand without first buying a token thesis.

## Alternates considered

1. **"…open-source environmental DePIN that turns low-cost Trees into a Chia-verified network for
   real-world data, uptime rewards, and citizen science."** — Clear, faithful to the current site,
   strong for crypto-native users. *Risk:* "DePIN" may age poorly / read insider by 2036.
2. **"…privacy-preserving network of community-planted Trees that transforms local environmental
   readings into verifiable public infrastructure."** — Cleanest institutional sentence; foregrounds
   privacy and public value. *Risk:* underplays Chia, $JUICE, and the reward system.
3. **"…open hardware-to-data layer for environmental sensing: Trees collect the world's signals,
   Chia verifies them, and $JUICE rewards the people who keep the network alive."** — Most product
   architecture embedded; easy-to-grasp loop. *Risk:* "hardware-to-data layer" is abstract for a
   first line; "world's signals" needs care before the network is large.
