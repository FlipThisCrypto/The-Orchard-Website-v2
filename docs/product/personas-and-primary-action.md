# Personas & the Primary Action

> (`council/chatgpt/submissions/ORCH-011-v1.md`). Date: 2026-06-17.

## Primary persona (launch): the crypto-native operator-builder
A technically curious Chia/DePIN participant who understands wallets, open-source repos, ESP32
hardware, pre-alpha rough edges, and earning $JUICE for keeping real infrastructure online. They
come first because the bottleneck isn't institutional demand — it's **credible network growth from
~3 Trees** toward enough live Trees/Groves to make Orchard View, the globe, and a future data
commons meaningful. Hardware-first → convert the rare visitor who can actually plant, test, break,
report, and improve a Tree.

## Ranked personas
1. **Crypto-native operator-builder** — *primary for launch.* Plants, verifies, earns, iterates; tolerates pre-alpha if the path is honest. Most likely to solve cold-start.
2. **Open-source / citizen-science tester** — builds, forks, teaches, deploys locally; may not care about $JUICE; contributes drivers/docs/field-testing. Same primary path, token language not dominant.
3. **Curious explorer / observer** — wants to understand the globe and whether this is real; becomes a follower/contributor/future operator if the network is legible in <1 minute.
4. **Research / city / farm / insurer / environmental group** — *later evaluator/buyer.* Wants provenance, privacy posture, coverage, exportability, governance. Secondary at launch (network not yet at their scale). Must not be forced through crypto-native language.

## The single 60-second action: **"Start a Tree readiness check"**
Within 60 seconds a first-timer should answer *"Can I plant a Tree, and what's my next step?"* The
action routes into a lightweight checklist — hardware on hand, supported board, sensors,
wallet/Orchard Pass status, comfort with pre-alpha — then the concrete next path (flash firmware,
claim a Tree, read build docs, or follow/join for later).

Why this beats the alternatives: the **globe** is the proof surface and emotional hook but the
network needs more Trees; **docs** are necessary but passive; **$JUICE** matters but the token
supports the ecosystem, it isn't the point. A readiness check keeps the funnel hardware-first,
honest, and useful to current operators *and* later evaluators.

## High-level journeys
- **Operator-builder:** globe → sees it's live but early → opens a Tree/Grove example → "Start
  readiness check" → confirms hardware/wallet → flash/claim, or gets a precise missing-step list.
- **Data evaluator:** globe → inspects public Tree/Grove data (coarse location) → uptime, sensors,
  history, DataLayer proof, device health in the Explorer → next step is "watch it mature" or
  "request/export sample data" later (not procurement yet).
- **Curious explorer:** no Chia/hardware context → explores the globe → learns fruit = data classes
  and the loop (plant → Harvest → prove uptime → earn $JUICE) → follow / star repo / save the build path.
