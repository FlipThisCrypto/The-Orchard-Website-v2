## Dominant narratives right now
- **AI DePIN / AI×Crypto is the loudest lane** — decentralized compute/bandwidth for AI; projects (e.g., Akash) judged on **utilization**, not just narrative price ("the market is pricing the narrative"). Bandwidth plays (Grass) framed as fixing centralized AI's free data use.
- **Verification + real-world proof is rising** — e.g., behavior-verification infra using "Proof of Human Action," privacy-first (hashes on-chain, raw data local), ESG/carbon outputs, language like *"not telling stories — recording what actually happened."*
- **"Airdrop farming is dead" fatigue** — coexists with excitement for projects showing real deployment + unit economics; low-proof incentives read as dated.
- **Grassroots community infrastructure vs. centralized** — DePIN's edge where legacy systems leave gaps.
- **Environmental/sensor data is a *quiet* lane** vs. AI/compute/wireless — **an opening for distinct positioning.**

## Tactics getting attention
- Light gamification tied to **real actions** (prediction games/quests with transparent rules + real utility).
- Operator-focused onboarding + **proof-of-deployment** trust signals over abstract promises.
- Early benefits for **genuine contribution** (device-days, uptime, events) — not holding/low-bar farming.
- Narrative alignment with macro (AI demand, carbon/ESG, industrial digitalization).
- **Utilization & proof as the story** ("here's the actual verified work," not "share idle X and earn").

## Borrow (mapped to our pillars)
1. **Pillar 1 (verifiable data):** double down on signatures + Chia DataLayer as clean "on-chain verification"; use real uptime proofs + Explorer data in threads; the globe is a powerful "show the actual network" surface.
2. **Pillar 2 (infrastructure-first):** lean into "record what actually happened" — Season attestation + verified contributions over airdrop promises (already aligned with farming fatigue).
3. **Pillar 3 (forkable):** emphasize open-source + low-bar ESP32 entry as the contrast to proprietary/high-bar setups when AI-DePIN talk turns to "bypassing legacy monopolies."
- **Specific:** transparent contribution challenges *only if they produce real sensor data or code*; recognition for operators who generate verifiable value; connect to ESG/compliance language **where it fits naturally**.

## Avoid
- "Idle resource / easy earn" framing (drawing the "airdrop is dead" pushback).
- Over-indexing on the AI/compute narrative and diluting our environmental category — we're complementary infra, not another GPU/bandwidth play.
- Pure hype cycles; abstract "join Discord for a role and points" with no real Tree/contribution.
- Multi-token / fragmented stories — stick to single-$JUICE-supports-real-work.

## Reconciliation
